A Critique on Browsers

At Peter's last night, I ripped into every web browser for lack of evolution in their features. Granted, some browsers have implemented several of these suggestions, but none have them all.

Look at your list of bookmarks now. Are they organised into folders, sorted by last access? They ought to be. I don't know of any studies regarding bookmark behaviour, but I am certain that you click most often on the most recently added bookmarks.

Do they synchronise to your mobile or export as HTML? They ought to. I'd like to be able to carry mine wherever I go.

Can you set them to delete automatically on a certain date? For instance, if I bookmark an evite or an eBay auction, the system ought to know that these are time-dependant things, and so would scan them for an end-date and automatically delete the bookmark within a certain period after the end date. Using eBay's REST API, this should not be too difficult to do. When Evite unveils an API of their own, getting the invitation date should be simple. In fact, they do allow generation of vCalendar format events.

Do they have change detection? OmniWeb does change detection using the hash of the page's content. It does not resort the bookmarks menu, putting the most recently-updated bookmarks on top, though.

I ought to be able to search through the content of my session history. Most browsers will display a list of pages that were last accessed. This should evolve into something more useful. Using lucene, one can index the actual text on the page and have it searchable. This way you won't need to go looking for that bookmark that had the bit on that brand of shoes that Priscilla thought would be nice to get on her upcoming birthday.

The way history is displayed ought to change, from a text-based menu to a graph, where I can actually see how I came to arrive at a given page.

Oh, and might I add this should be platform-independant? It's all possible, just a matter of sitting down and doing it.
